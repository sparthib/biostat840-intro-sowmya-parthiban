example_dataset

Diabetes is a prevelant disorder that affects people from various backgrounds and across all age groups and genders. In females however, it is know to cause hormonal imbalance which can lead to pregnancy related issues (McCance 2011), (Berger, Gagnon, and Sermer 2016), (Simmons 2011) .

The PIMA diabetes dataset was originally created to predict whether a person has diabetes or not. All individuals in the dataset are over 21, and females and are of PIMA Indian descent. This page aims to do some preliminary analysis of some variables before they can be used for further exploration. This project can be interesting to anyone who is trying to explore factors related to diabetes in females. The data and dictionary for the datset can be found here.

Questions I am asking are:

How goes glucose level relate to age or age_group.

What is the distribution of the number of pregnancies across different age groups.

Convert age to age_range

df <- df |> mutate(
Age_range = case_when(
Age > 19 & Age <= 29 ~ "20-29",
Age > 29 & Age <= 39 ~ "30-39",
Age > 39 & Age <= 49 ~ "40-49",
Age > 49 & Age <= 59 ~ "50-59",
Age > 59 & Age <= 69 ~ "60-69",
Age > 69 & Age <= 79 ~ "70-79",
Age > 79 & Age <= 89 ~ "80-89",
Age > 89 & Age <= 99 ~ "90-99" ),
#convert to factor
Age_range = factor(
Age_range, level = c("20-29", "30-39", "40-49", "50-59",
"60-69", "70-79", "80-89", "90-99")

))
#make sure the age ranges are what we want

Converting Age_range to factors will make it easier to facet by it later while making plots

Trends in glucose level with age.

ggplot(df, aes(x=Age, y=Glucose)) +
  geom_point(size=2)

View the distribution of glucose levels per age group using box plots.

 ggplot(df, aes(x= Age_range, y= Glucose))+ geom_boxplot()

Make a histogram of number of pregnancies by age group.

#| label: histogram
df_preg <- df |>  group_by(Age_range, Pregnancies) |> summarise(freq = n())
`summarise()` has grouped output by 'Age_range'. You can override using the
`.groups` argument.
ggplot(df_preg, aes(x = Pregnancies, y = freq )) + geom_bar(stat = "identity")+
    facet_wrap(~Age_range)
